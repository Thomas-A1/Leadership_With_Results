SDS Case Overview: Who We Are and What's Wrong

Company Profile

SDS: Ghanaian leader in digital government platforms and AI analytics across West Africa.

Known for technical expertise, organizational agility, and client-focused innovation over the last decade.

The Trigger

Sankofa Insights 4.0 significantly delayed

Advanced analytics suite for high-profile public sector agencies

Board pressure emphasizing speed over strategic clarity

Core Issue

Not technical capacity — leadership behaviors

Leadership styles and emotional intelligence deficits harming organizational climate and performance outcomes (Goleman, 2017).

Key Symptoms

Organizational Structure

• Strategic ambiguity; unclear vision for Insights 4.0

• Unclear roles and responsibilities across departments

Team Dynamics

• Inconsistent expectations between departments

• Low psychological safety; fear of reprimand

Staff Experience

• Engineers hesitate to experiment; fear mistakes

• Data scientists overwhelmed by unrealistic expectations

• Designers excluded from decision-making processes

What's Missing: Authoritative (Visionary) and Coaching

A

Authoritative (Visionary)

"Come with me" approach

Why SDS needs this now:

Engineers cite "inconsistent direction and shifting priorities." The current leadership vacuum leaves teams without a clear North Star or stable roadmap for Insights 4.0.

Key benefits for SDS:

• Provides clear North Star and alignment across teams

• Converts pressure into purpose; stabilizes priorities

• Increases clarity and commitment during final development

• Allows flexibility in execution while maintaining direction

Impact on climate drivers:

Clarity ↑↑ Commitment ↑↑ Flexibility ↑ Standards ↑

(Goleman, 2017)

C

Coaching

"Try this" approach

Why SDS needs this now:

Staff report "mentoring and coaching are absent despite evolving technical requirements." Engineers hesitate to experiment, limiting innovation and ownership.

Key benefits for SDS:

• Builds capacity amid evolving requirements

• Restores mentoring and 1:1 support

• Improves autonomy, reduces burnout

• Creates safe space for experimentation and growth

Impact on climate drivers:

Responsibility ↑↑ Commitment ↑ Standards ↑ Flexibility ↑

(Goleman, 2017)

Net effect: Replace fear and fragmentation with direction, growth, and coordinated execution

Emotional Intelligence Deficits

Self-Awareness Deficiency

Leaders unaware of how their behaviors impact the organization; lack understanding of their own emotional triggers and effects on others.

Need:

Understanding of own emotions, recognition of how actions affect others, and ability to adjust leadership styles appropriately.

Impact:

Prevents leaders from developing other EI competencies and adapting leadership approaches effectively (Goleman, 2017).

Self-Management Needed

CTO reacts with micromanagement; CEO reacts with coercion.

Need:

Strategic response vs. reaction, emotional control, and stress management.

Impact:

Creates reactive, inconsistent leadership that undermines stability and prevents teams from developing effective working patterns (Goleman, 2017).

Social Awareness Required

Leaders disconnected from staff needs; lack empathy for burnout & concerns.

Need:

Active listening, perspective-taking, and understanding staff emotional states.

Impact:

Disconnect between leadership behaviors and staff needs creates cycle of deteriorating morale and prevents building relationships necessary for collaboration (Goleman, 2017).

Social Skills Deficiency

HR & Innovation Lead avoid conflicts; poor communication & coordination.

Need:

Conflict management capabilities, clear messaging, and effective facilitation.

Impact:

Prevents effective leadership style adaptation and coordination, exacerbating organizational problems (Goleman, 2017).

Recommended Leadership Style Transformation

CEO: Authoritative + Affiliative

Shift away from coercive, deadline-centered approach. Acknowledge current pressures and effort invested. Re-articulate vision for SDS's aspirations, allowing executive team feedback. Develop social awareness and social skills. This de-escalates organizational pressure and creates space for candid communication about capacity and timeline adjustments (Goleman, 2017).

CTO: Authoritative + Coaching

Adopt authoritative approach to ensure consistent direction across Design, Data Science, and Engineering teams. Shift from doing technical work to coaching and developing team capabilities. Cultivate growth mindset. Develop self-management and social awareness. Coaching fosters employees' belief in their capacity to continuously rise to challenges (Goleman, 2017).

HR & Innovation Lead: Strengthen Social Skills

Rather than avoiding conflict, strengthen communication and conflict-management capabilities. Become proficient in listening, delivering clear and well-tailored messages, resolving disagreements, and facilitating solutions effectively. Clear messaging enables employees to act decisively with firm sense of purpose (Goleman, 2017).

90-Day Priority Action Plan

Immediate (Days 1-30)

  • Emergency leadership team meeting: CEO acknowledges pressures, solicits feedback
  • Executive-Board meeting to discuss company pressures and timeline adjustments
  • Customer engagement to explore possible timeline adjustment
  • CEO company-wide communication acknowledging pressures and outlining adjustments

Short-term (Days 31-60)

  • Internal company-climate audit led by HR Director and co-led by CEO
  • Internal structural adjustments for Engineering, Data Science, and Design teams
  • EI development workshops focusing on self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, and social skills

Mid-term (Days 61-90)

  • Reinforce new leadership behaviors through regular check-ins
  • Measure climate improvements using Goleman's climate drivers
  • Celebrate progress and early wins to build momentum
